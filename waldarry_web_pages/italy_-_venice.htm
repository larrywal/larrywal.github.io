VENICE (VENEZIA)

We left early so we could arrive in Venice and have most of the day to sightsee. Our hotel was newly renovated and advertised air conditioners in all rooms, however, they never specified that all were working! They weren’t, however, Joyce was able to arrange to stay in the cooler room.

Our hotel was near Piazza San Marco and that was the first place we went. Our unique walking tour took us past the Campanile, Palazzo Ducale, Ponte dei Sospiri, the Bronze Horses, Grand Canal, and the Rialto Bridge to the Jewish Quarter. Fortunately, the further we walked from Piazza San Marco, the more we avoided the throngs of tourists. On our return walk we decided to take a gondola ride. What a perfect way to get the complete perspective of a city…on foot and by gondola.

It happened that our gondolier was also an identical twin. He gave us a historical perspective of the city and also pointed out the former homes of Marco Polo and Mozart. We saw a gondola full of Hewlett Packard printers being unloaded and Bob felt right at home.
